Challenge

The challenge was organized as part of the Statistical Modelling and Computational Modeling of the Heart (STACOM) 2012 workshop held at MICCAI. STACOM is supported by the cardiac atlas project. MICCAI 2012 was its 15th International Conference on Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention, and was held from 1st to 5th October 2012 in Nice, France, organised by INRIA (French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics).

Participants

Five institutions took part in the organized challenge. Two of these institutions were commerical software providers. The participants were: 1) Department of Imaging Sciences and Biomedical Engineering at King's college London in United Kingdom. 2) Center for Computational Imaging and Simulation Technologies in Biomedicine at Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, Spain. 3) Alma IT systems in Barcelona, Spain. 4) Center for intelligence machine at McGill University in Canada. 5) Fraunhofer Institute for Medical Image Computing, Fraunhofer MEVIS in Germany.

Datasets

The image database consisted of 30 multicenter, multivendor LGE CMR images of both humans and porcine. The human patient dataset was acquired at 1.5T and consisted of patients with known history of ischaemic cardiomyopathy and under assessment for implantable cardioverter defibrillator (ICD) for primary and secondary preventions after infarction. The porcine dataset was acquired at 3T and consisted of images with lesions induced using the left-anterior descending or left-circum ex artery.
