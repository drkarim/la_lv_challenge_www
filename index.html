Atrial wall segmentation

Non-invasive 3-dimensional imaging using CTA can accurately provide information about the left atrium wall thickness. New sequence design has also enabled acquisition of the LA wall thickness using CMR. The reliability of these parameters need to be determined prior to evaluating the clinical utility.

Atrial fibrosis and scar quantification

This completed study determined that no fibrosis algorithm was deemed better than others. The algorithms when analysed for overlaps with psuedo ground truth, produced Dice scores far from optimal. There is much scope for further algorithmic developments in LA fibrosis and scar quantification from LGE CMR images.

Ventricle infarct segmentation

This completed study looked at algorithms for quantifying infarct in the LV from LGE CMR. An extensive analysis was performed following the completion of the challenge. Common algorithms such as FWHM and standard-deviations are scrutinised on both animal and patient datatsets, along-side advanced image processing techniques.

Left ventricle MRI infarct benchmark.

A benchmarking platform for myocardial infarct segmentation algorithms of the left ventricle. The image database consists of 30 multi-center, multi-vendor, and multi-resolution datasets.

Left atrium MRI scar and fibrosis benchmarks.

A benchmarking platform for atrial fibrosis and scar segmentation algorithms. The image database consists of 60 multi-center, multi-vendor, and multi-resolution datasets.

Standardised evaluation of future algorithms.

Both benchmarks provide pseudo ground truths obtained from 3 or more observers using maximum likelihood estimation, evaluation strategies and algorithm ranking.
